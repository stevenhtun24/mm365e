Microsoft Teams: the collaboration tool your business needs

Effective workplace communication is at the heart of all businesses. A collaborative workspace fosters a sense of community amongst employees – allowing them to easily create relationships while sharing resources and other information with their co-workers and stakeholders.

Whether you are a small business with less than ten staff, or a larger organisation with numerous departments, teamwork and communication inform every decision you make, and you need to have the right collaboration platform to ensure that you never lose momentum.

Microsoft Teams is one such application that companies have used to keep their staff connected and communicating effectively.



What is Microsoft Teams?





Microsoft Teams (also known as Teams) is an all-purpose collaboration tool included in the M365 suite. Besides being able to integrate with over 700 applications, Microsoft Teams blends the real-time chat features of social media with the file sharing and collaboration software of project management tools.

Regardless of the sector you work in or the specifics of your daily tasks, Teams is a platform meant to facilitate communication at work and prevent your business from suffering from ineffective communication and delayed results. According to Deloitte, businesses that emphasise the importance of collaborative work are five times more likely to grow their employee base.

Whether you conduct business in a physical work environment, as a hybrid, or remote company, keeping workers connected despite distances and clashing schedules can be a struggle. Communication tools such as Teams can minimise the strain this places upon your business by giving you the ability to easily coordinate your staff and projects as if you were in the office.
